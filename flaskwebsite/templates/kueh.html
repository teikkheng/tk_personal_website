What are Kuehs?

Kuehs are a sweet or savoury bite-sized snacks, with its roots traced back to Malay and Indonesian cuisines. They are made with traditional techniques and authentic ingredients. Here are 15 traditional kuehs that are commonly found in Singapore:


Pulut Inti

Made with steamed glutinous rice that have been infused with sweet coconut milk. Shaped like a pyramid, the Pulut Inti has it’s tip packed with grated coconut that is cooked with cane sugar and jawa/palm sugar.


Tutu Kueh

Little steamed rice-flour cakes filled with delicious fillings such as peanut, coconut and occasionally chocolate or gula melaka.


Kueh Pie Tee

A popular Peranakan dish, it is simply a thin and crispy pastry tart shell filled with a spicy, sweet mixture of thinly sliced vegetables and prawns.


Putu Ayu

This beautiful steamed treat, shaped like a mini bundt cake, is essentially green pandan sponge cake crowned with shredded coconut.


Talam Ubi

This delicious cake has two layers. The top layer is made from coconut milk, and the bottom layer of the cake is made with grated cassava and is flavored with palm sugar.


Ondeh Ondeh

Made from either sweet potato or glutinous rice flour. These cute little balls are infused with pandan juice and filled with gula Melaka or palm sugar before being rolled in some freshly grated coconut.


Lemper Udang

Made from glutinous rice, infused with coconut milk and flavour from pandan leaves. While the glutinous rice is steaming, grated coconut will be fried in a dry wok to bring out its fragrance before being added as a filling into the glutinous rice and lastly, wrapped in banana leaves.


Kueh Lapis

Easiest to recognize and remember due to its colourful layers, some people call this “thousand-layer cake” but it actually only has 9 layers. It is made with tapioca, rice flour and coconut milk before being steamed.


Kueh Salat

A two-tiered dessert with a base of steamed glutinous rice weighted down by a thick layer of custard flavoured with freshly squeezed coconut milk and pandan juice.


Kueh Talam

Not to be confused with Kueh Salat though their colours look almost similar. It also has two layers, a top white layer made from coconut milk and rice flour and a bottom green layer made from rice and mung bean flour with natural green colouring from pandan leaves.


Kueh Kosui

Made from tapioca starch and rice flour and sweetened with gula Melaka and sugar. Pandan leaves enhance the fragrance of this kueh and it is usually served with freshly grated coconuts for more texture and taste.


Kueh Dadar

It has juicy palm sugar flavoured grated coconut fillings enveloped within a piece of green crepe. Pandan leaf is the core ingredient of Kueh Dadar and the green exterior is made of a batter coloured with natural pandan juice extracted from pandan leaves, hence the tempting sweet fragrance.


Bingka Ubi

This baked tapioca cake is fragrant, semi-soft, slightly chewy and goes perfectly well when paired with a cup of tea. It has an inviting aroma from the pandan leaves, eggs and coconut milk hence its rich and flavourful taste.


Harum Manis

A steamed sponge cake embellished with slices of bananas. The steamed cakes are enriched by the warm caramel-like sweetness from gula Melaka and wafts of aroma from the cooked bananas.


Ang Ku Kueh

A small round or oval shaped pastry with soft sticky glutinous rice flour skin wrapped around a sweet filling in the centre. It is moulded to resemble a tortoise shell and is presented resting on a square piece of banana leaf. The common and original fillings are sweet bean paste and peanuts.
